PerkinElmer

Co-op terms W20 and S20



Introduction

My name is Michael Vamvakas, I am a fifth year Software Engineering Student with a minor in Mathematics. This page is a reflection of my second 8 month work (January 2020 - August 2020) at Perkin Elmer, this time working as an Associate Software Development Engineer. In this report I will talk about the numerous ways I grew as a Student, Employee, and Programmer.



Employer Information

PerkinElmer is an American company founded in 1939. They focus in the areas of human and environmental health. This includes environmental analysis, food and consumer product safety, and life science research to name a few. The software, medical imaging components, instruments, and consumables help many end markets. Some of these markets are Animal Health, Food & Agriculture, and Therapeutic Areas. Their offices are located all over the world, including the United States, Canada, France, South Africa, Japan, Australia, China, and Brazil. The Guelph R&D Team is currently working on informatics software for the medical industry.



Job Description

While working at PerkinElmer as an Associate Software Development Engineer, my main job was to assist in fixing bugs and implementing new features for Signals Medical Review. My role within the team was to either work on bugs assigned to me using Jira, or work with other developers to implement new features/stories discussed during sprint planning.

Many of the things I did at PerkinElmer Include:
- Fixing bugs/errors in Signals Medical Review - Implementing new features in Signals Medical Review - Writing unit tests for new code implemented.
- Using Jira, creating new tasks, assigning bugs/tasks from the backlog
- Programming in the .Net Framework
- Working with the Angular Framework



Goals

My main goals while working for PerkinElmer were to increase my abilities as a programmer, as well as grow within a professional environment. I wanted to work on my communication skills.

My goals for the first 4 months were:
1. Improve abilities with the .net framework
2. Learn more about how to properly use Agile
3. Be more active at attempting problems I don't know how to solve


As I had never worked with the .Net Framework before heading into this work term so my main goal during my first 4 months was to learn as much as I can about C#, Angular, and the .Net Framework as a whole. My other important goal was to make a greater effort to take on tasks I had no idea how to solve and trying to work through them. I also had a a goal to learn more about Agile as there is almost more to learn and improve when working with it.

My goals for the last 4 months were
1. Learn more about .Net
2. Stay motivated and focused while working from home
3. Increase my ability to problem solve

After the first four months I realized I still have a long way to go with the .Net Framework, so I wanted to continue focusing on learning as much as I can about it as I could. My secondary goal was to try to stay as motivated as possible while working from home. As I have ADD working from home full time and staying focused on task proved to be quite the challenge. It started out badly but as time went on I feel I adjusted to working from home quite well.

Reflecting on my goals and everything I've learned while working at PerkinElmer, I believe that I had an extremely successful work term. Despite the COVID-19 kicking me out of the office 2.5 months I feel I improved my programming skills a lot and had a great time working for PerkinElmer.
